# Twilight Archmage Regular Fight

Source page: [RealmEye - Twilight Archmage](https://www.realmeye.com/wiki/twilight-archmage)

## Attack Selection Rules

- Phase 1 introduces the core loop and the same fire/ice selection system continues through the fight.
- Three generators reroll as orange or blue whenever the boss starts charging.
- Fire majority means a fire pattern. Ice majority means an ice pattern.
- If all three generators match, the boss upgrades into a stronger nuke pattern for that element.
- The boss only takes damage while a pattern is active.
- If players push about 10% of his max HP during a live pattern, that pattern ends immediately and he starts charging the next one.
- The page says he alternates between the two available attacks for each attack type instead of repeating the last one used for the same condition.

## Phase 1: Element Roulette

- Threshold: start of fight to 70% HP.
- The Archmage channels power from the three generators and attacks from the center of the room.
- The page describes four fire patterns and four ice patterns total. It also states that two are majority attacks and two are nuke attacks, but the individual entries are not explicitly labeled on the page, so they are listed below in page order.

### Fire Pattern A

- Taunt: `Firos Xiflis'TOh Dristatiks Quantinoftus!`
- Five fire portals rotate clockwise on the room edge and fire inward to create a five-armed spiral.
- The boss periodically throws two six-shot rings of flame vortexes that inflict Sick and Bleeding.
- He also tosses Fire Bombs in rings of eight; the bombs explode after a short fuse.
- Bomb distances cycle between close, medium, and far throws.

### Fire Pattern B

- Taunt: `Firos Xiflis'TOh Gantsualo Quantinoftus!`
- Three fire portals rotate clockwise on a tighter orbit near the boss.
- Those portals pulse rings of accelerating fire waves and smaller fire blasts.
- The boss launches random Fire Bombs across the arena.
- He also fires small clockwise shotguns of flaming blasts.

### Fire Pattern C

- Taunt: `Firos Xiflis'TOh Wavunwavun Quantinoftus!`
- The boss sprays small fireballs in every direction on turning trajectories.
- He adds periodic three-way flame vortex spreads.
- Blue slowing spinners arrive from the arena walls in three-shot rows.
- Eight invincible fire portals rotate counterclockwise and pulse short-ranged fire shots.

### Fire Pattern D

- Taunt: `Firos Xiflis'TOh Pridigioc Quantinoftus!`
- The boss opens with two streams of fire waves on either the horizontal or vertical axis.
- Blue slowing spinners enter from the outside while eight fire portals rotate counterclockwise and fire short-range bursts that trap players.
- Dense armor-piercing fire-wave rows then sweep in from the two sides matching the initial axis.
- Safe gaps alternate between the two incoming walls, so players have to weave instead of standing still.
- The pattern ends with a large Fire Bomb dump around the boss.
- The axis alternates between horizontal and vertical the next time this pattern appears.

### Ice Pattern A

- Taunt: `Toahc Xifls'TOh Rigroatah Qualtinoc!`
- Ice portals spawn in pairs or quartets and move away in opposite directions.
- The portals repeatedly fire four-way armor-piercing ice-wave shotguns on cardinal or diagonal lines.
- The boss adds three faster rings of silencing Frost Twirls that rotate against each other.

### Ice Pattern B

- Taunt: `Toahc Xifls'TOh Puroktis Qualtinoc!`
- Many ice portals begin in a cross and then rotate in alternating directions into a dense moving lattice.
- The portals pulse silencing AoEs.
- They also fire smaller bolts and heavier frost-wave volleys in two directions; outer portals fire in four directions instead.
- The boss periodically launches slow blue stars in random directions.

### Ice Pattern C

- Taunt: `Toahc Xifls'TOh Nohsdrol Qualtinoc!`
- A stationary ring of icy spinners traps players while a second inner ring made of three clockwise rotating streaks stays near the boss.
- Six edge portals rotate counterclockwise, firing short-range ice blasts in all directions.
- Those same portals also fire synchronized accelerating purple spinners that converge into ring patterns.
- The boss rapidly fires beam-like volleys in multiple directions.
- One beam style combines an ice wave with several small bolts.
- The other beam style combines a larger ice wave with several ice blasts.
- These beams can appear in three, six, or eight directions at once.

### Ice Pattern D

- Taunt: `Toahc Xifls'TOh Merilmeril Qualtinoc!`
- Six ice portals pulse AoEs and fire slow clusters of icy twirls aimed at players.
- Their movement alternates between counterclockwise laps along the room edge and clockwise rotations nearer the center.
- The boss also spawns invincible Ice Spheres that grow and then burst into directed icicle spreads.

## Phase 2: First Generator Seal

- Threshold: 70% HP.
- The Archmage goes invulnerable and darkens the screen.
- The three Magi-Generators become vulnerable and attack with overlapping rings of purple spinners.
- Until one generator is destroyed, players are permanently Sick and Bleeding.
- The first generator defeated retracts into the floor and becomes sealed in its current element.
- That sealed generator then permanently contributes its locked element to later attack rolls.
- This lets players bias the fight toward fire or ice and can completely remove one element's nuke attack from the remaining phases.

## Phase 3: Single Bird Support

- The boss resumes normal attack selection, but now each pattern summons one bird: Inferno on fire attacks, Blizzard on ice attacks.
- While the active bird is alive, the boss is immune to damage.
- Players must kill the bird to stagger the Archmage.
- After the bird dies there is about a three-second grace window for old bullets to clear, then the boss is vulnerable for about twenty seconds.
- If the current pattern is a nuke, the matching bird becomes Armored.
- The birds can circle the room, orbit the generators with intermittent fly-bys, idle by a random generator, or travel the walkways depending on the pattern.
- The birds are marked on the minimap with orange or blue dots.
- When the boss is staggered, the defeated bird regenerates, becomes invulnerable, and still defends him during the damage window.
- Blizzard attacks by firing four-way streams of ice blasts and shotgun bursts of ice waves.
- Inferno attacks with dense shotguns of fireballs and fire waves fired in opposite directions.
- If players deal about 15% of the boss's max HP during a stagger, or push him into another checkpoint, he recovers early.
- Bird damage carries between consecutive same-element patterns if the bird was not finished on the previous one.

## Phase 4: Second Generator Seal

- Threshold: 40% HP.
- This repeats the generator-seal phase from 70%.
- The currently active bird also stays on the map, invulnerable, and keeps firing while the generators are up.
- If players end up with one fire generator and one ice generator sealed by this point, no future nuke attacks can occur.

## Phase 5: Dual Bird Support

- Both Inferno and Blizzard are active for every pattern.
- Both birds must be killed before the boss can be staggered and damaged.
- During the damage window, both birds return in invulnerable form and keep defending him, though with reduced fire rate.
- The bird with elemental disadvantage is permanently Exposed during this phase.
- If the boss is using a nuke, the off-element bird becomes Armor Broken instead while the matching bird becomes Armored.
- If only one bird dies before the pattern ends, the surviving bird keeps its remaining damage but the dead bird respawns at full HP on the next pattern.

## Phase 6: Final Generator Seal

- Threshold: 10% HP.
- The last remaining Magi-Generator becomes vulnerable.
- Both birds stay active and support the invulnerable boss while players try to seal the final generator.
- Once the final generator is sealed, the fight advances immediately.

## Phase 7: Generator-Lock Bird Duel

- The Archmage heals back to 20% HP, turns invulnerable, and channels with both orange and blue energy active.
- Inferno and Blizzard use one of two special attack scripts depending on which element has at least two locked generators.

### If Ice Has The Locked Majority

- Inferno is the first vulnerable bird.
- Blizzard circles tightly around the Archmage and fires eight-way chains of ice shots.
- Inferno circles the room edge and throws dense rings of small fireballs.
- Inferno sometimes cuts into the middle and adds rings of larger fireballs plus short fire-wave pulses.
- After Inferno dies, Blizzard becomes Armored, turns vulnerable, and chases players.
- In that chase state Blizzard uses three-way shot-chain bursts and stops periodically for a spiral of icy twirls.

### If Fire Has The Locked Majority

- Blizzard is the first vulnerable bird.
- Inferno circles close to the Archmage and fires dense multilayered rings of armor-piercing fire blasts at players.
- Blizzard chases players with wavy bolts, side ice twirls, aimed ice-blast shotguns, and rings of icy spinners.
- After Blizzard dies, Inferno becomes Armored, turns vulnerable, and adds spiral Fire Bomb throws on top of its normal attacks.

- Both birds must die before the boss becomes vulnerable.

## Phase 8: Finale

- The final desperation pattern depends on which bird died last in Phase 7, and therefore which element holds generator majority.
- For the first ten seconds the boss is Invulnerable.
- For the next ten seconds he remains Armored.

### Fire Finale

- The room is divided vertically with lines of fire waves.
- Eight fire portals rotate counterclockwise around the perimeter and fire short-ranged shots that make the edge unsafe.
- Each large line splits into many smaller fire-wave segments.
- Those segments orbit at different radii, creating a rotating lattice that players have to weave through.
- The boss also keeps throwing Fire Bombs and pulsing short-range fire waves.

### Ice Finale

- Eight ice portals rotate counterclockwise on the outer edge and fire an extremely dense random barrage of icy twirls and vortexes inward.
- They also fire ice-bolt spreads backward to prevent hiding behind them.
- The boss periodically throws Ice Spheres that land and explode into directed shard spreads.

## Death And Aftermath

- On death, the Archmage collapses, his orb turns into a dark purple vortex, and he is pulled into it.
- Loot drops where he stood.
- The way into the Grand Hall opens after the fight ends.
