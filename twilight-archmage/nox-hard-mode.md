# Nox The Wild Shadow

Source page: [RealmEye - Twilight Archmage](https://www.realmeye.com/wiki/twilight-archmage)

## Unlock Condition

- Destroy The Source before starting the fight.
- Instead of activating three generators, players must activate four.
- When the boss wakes with all four generators online, he reveals his true name, Nox the Wild Shadow, and the fight upgrades into hard mode.

## Global Changes

- Nox keeps all regular Twilight Archmage elemental attacks.
- Because the arena now uses four generators, he gains three extra selection outcomes:
- All four fire generators
- All four ice generators
- A two-fire / two-ice balance state
- The page notes that Nox always opens by choosing all fire or all ice.
- Nox also adds Tempest later in the fight as a third support bird.

## Phase 1

- Same basic element-roulette structure as the normal fight, plus three new attack patterns.

### Four-Fire Pattern

- Taunt: `Firos Xiflis'TOh Oolarin Quantinoftus!`
- This is a diagonal version of the regular fire wall-weave pattern.
- Nox fires two diagonal streams of fire waves.
- Blue slowing spinners arrive from the arena outside.
- Eight fire portals rotate counterclockwise on the perimeter and fire short bursts to keep players boxed in.
- Dense diagonal rows of armor-piercing fire waves then sweep across the room from alternating sides with moving safe gaps.
- Nox ends the pattern with a large Fire Bomb dump and also throws extra bomb rings while the wall rows are still active.
- The attack alternates between the two diagonal axes on repeat appearances.

### Four-Ice Pattern

- Taunt: `Toahc Xifls'TOh Londovuk Qualtinoc!`
- Four ice portals orbit the perimeter.
- They fire dense bullet spreads outward, frost vortexes inward to create a converging spiral, and leave trail shots behind them.
- Nox repeatedly fires wavy two-direction streams.
- He also throws invincible Ice Spheres that later burst into directed ice-shard spreads.
- The portals periodically contract inward to trap players close to him, then expand again.
- While contracted, they stop firing frost vortexes.

### Balanced Two-Fire / Two-Ice Pattern

- Taunt: `Equim Xiflis'TOh Twamik Qualtinefin!`
- Nox first telegraphs with two silencing purple-spinner spreads.
- He then extends two huge bars of flame bursts that stall and begin rotating in a random direction.
- Sixteen portals orbit near the arena edge in opposite directions.
- Eight fire portals spray flames outward.
- Eight ice portals fire a messy inward mix of icy fireballs and frost vortexes.
- Nox also emits short fire shockwaves and throws both Fire Bombs and Ice Spheres.
- He periodically refreshes the rotating flame bars, changing their spin direction each time.
- Because neither element has advantage here, birds are neither buffed nor debuffed during this pattern.

## Phase 2

- Threshold: 80% HP.
- The page says this is identical to the regular Archmage Phase 2 generator-seal checkpoint, just earlier.

## Phase 3

- The page says this matches the regular Phase 3 bird phase.

## Phase 4

- Threshold: 60% HP.
- The page says this is identical to regular Phase 3, but the checkpoint timing suggests it functions as the second mid-fight checkpoint in the hard-mode route.

## Phase 5

- The page says this matches the regular dual-bird Phase 5.

## Phase 6

- Threshold: 40% HP.
- This starts like the regular final generator-seal checkpoint, but after the third Magi-Generator is destroyed Nox transitions into a new phase instead of continuing the normal script.

## Phase 7

- Nox summons a third bird, Tempest, shown on the minimap with a purple dot.
- All three birds must be killed to stagger him.
- Tempest has lower HP than Inferno or Blizzard.
- Tempest does not participate in the elemental buff/debuff system used by the other two birds.
- When Nox is staggered, Tempest reappears invulnerable and chases players while firing rings of stagnating stars that inflict Paralyzed.

## Phase 8

- Threshold: 20% HP.
- Nox goes invulnerable and the final generator becomes vulnerable.
- All three birds attack players during this checkpoint.
- Once the last generator is sealed, the fight continues.

## Phase 9

- This is a fixed bird-gauntlet phase instead of the generator-majority branching used in the normal fight.
- Inferno and Blizzard start vulnerable.
- Tempest is invulnerable until the other two birds are dead.
- The phase ends when Tempest dies.

### Inferno In Phase 9

- Circles the room, alternating between close and far orbits around the Archmage.
- Repeatedly fires rings of slow fireballs while moving.

### Blizzard In Phase 9

- Chases players.
- Fires spreads of ice blasts.
- Fires icy fireballs and bolts perpendicular to its flight path.
- Fires large ice spinners in random directions.
- Occasionally stops to spin in place and release a spiral of icy fireballs.

### Tempest In Phase 9

- Orbits the Archmage while firing three-way slowing purple beams that stagnate after a delay.
- After Inferno and Blizzard die, Tempest becomes Armored and starts chasing players.
- In chase mode it upgrades to a five-way spread and adds rings of stagnating Paralyze stars.

## Phase 10

- If the locked generators still have an elemental majority, the final phase follows the regular Archmage finale for that element.
- If the lock state is exactly two fire and two ice, Nox instead uses a special Balance Finale.

### Balance Finale

- Taunt: `Balance, that's it! I'VE DONE IT, MY LORD!`
- Nox draws a line of fire waves through the room.
- Eight ice portals rotate counterclockwise on the perimeter and fire short-ranged shots outward, making the edge unsafe.
- The fire-wave line splits into many smaller rotating segments, forming a circular moving lattice across the arena.
- Nox alternates between a dense inward-converging ring of ice stars and a dense outward-expanding ring, repeatedly forcing players toward and away from center.
- He also throws Fire Bombs and emits short-ranged fire waves.
- The page says Nox must use this specific attack as his last attack in order to unlock the hard-mode Forgotten King.

## Death, Rewards, And Outcome

- If Nox does not use the Balance Finale, his death scene is similar to the regular Archmage version.
- If he does use Balance Finale, he melts away instead of being sucked into the purple vortex.
- Defeating Nox drops one extra loot bag compared with the regular fight.
- More Accursed enemies appear in the Grand Hall afterward.
- The hard-mode final fight unlocks if Nox used Balance Finale before dying.
